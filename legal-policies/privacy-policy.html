Privacy Policy

Disciplefy: AI-Powered Bible Study Application

Effective Date: September 10, 2025
Last Updated: September 10, 2025
Quick Summary: Disciplefy is committed to protecting your privacy. We collect minimal personal information, use industry-standard security measures, and give you control over your data. You can use our app anonymously or create an account for enhanced features.

1. Introduction

Welcome to Disciplefy ("we," "our," or "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use our AI-powered Bible study application and related services (collectively, the "Service").

Disciplefy is designed to help Christians deepen their faith through AI-generated Bible study guides. We are committed to protecting your privacy while providing a meaningful spiritual experience.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information You Provide

  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your email address, name, and chosen authentication method (Google, Apple ID)
  • Profile Preferences: Language preference (English, Hindi, Malayalam), theme settings, and app preferences
  • Study Content: Bible verses, spiritual topics, and study guides you generate or save
  • Feedback: Comments, ratings, and suggestions you provide about our service
  • Payment Information: When purchasing tokens, we collect transaction details through our secure payment processor (Razorpay)

2.2 Information Automatically Collected

  • Usage Data: App interactions, features used, study guide generation frequency
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, app version, device identifiers
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, session duration, error logs
  • Analytics Data: Aggregated usage patterns and app performance metrics

2.3 Anonymous Usage

You can use Disciplefy without creating an account. Anonymous users receive temporary session identifiers and are subject to usage limits (10 study guides per hour).

4. How We Use Your Information

4.1 Primary Uses

  • Service Delivery: Generate AI-powered Bible study guides tailored to your preferences
  • Account Management: Maintain your account, preferences, and saved content
  • Personalization: Customize the app experience based on your language and usage patterns
  • Communication: Send important service updates and respond to your inquiries

4.2 AI and Content Generation

We use your input (Bible verses, spiritual topics) to generate study guides through artificial intelligence services. Your input is:

  • Processed securely through encrypted connections
  • Used solely for generating your study content
  • Not used to train AI models or shared with AI providers for training purposes
  • Subject to content filtering to ensure appropriate, biblical content

4.3 Analytics and Improvement

  • Service Enhancement: Analyze usage patterns to improve app functionality
  • Performance Monitoring: Track app performance and identify technical issues
  • Security: Monitor for suspicious activity and protect against abuse

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

5.1 We DO NOT Sell Your Data

We never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

5.2 Service Providers

We may share information with trusted service providers who help operate our service:

  • Supabase: Database hosting and authentication services
  • OpenAI/Anthropic: AI services for study guide generation (input only, not stored)
  • Razorpay: Payment processing for token purchases
  • Analytics Providers: Aggregated usage statistics (no personal identification)

5.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or to:

  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Protect our rights and property
  • Ensure user safety and security
  • Investigate potential violations of our Terms of Service

6. Data Security

6.1 Security Measures

  • Encryption: All data transmitted using TLS 1.3 encryption
  • Database Security: Row-level security and access controls
  • Authentication: Secure OAuth 2.0 implementation
  • Access Control: Limited employee access on need-to-know basis
  • Regular Audits: Ongoing security assessments and updates

6.2 Data Storage

  • Location: Data stored in secure, compliant cloud infrastructure
  • Backup: Regular encrypted backups with geographic distribution
  • Retention: Data retained only as long as necessary for service provision

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Account Controls

  • Access: View and download your personal data
  • Update: Modify your profile information and preferences
  • Delete: Remove specific study guides or your entire account
  • Export: Download your data in a portable format

7.2 Privacy Settings

  • Analytics Opt-out: Disable usage tracking (may affect service quality)
  • Communication Preferences: Control email notifications
  • Data Sharing: Manage data sharing with service providers where possible

7.3 GDPR Rights (EU Users)

If you are located in the European Union, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right of Access: Obtain confirmation and details about data processing
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Object to certain types of data processing
  • Right to Restrict: Limit how we process your data

8. Children's Privacy

Disciplefy is designed for users aged 13 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under 13, we will take immediate steps to delete that information.

Parents and guardians are encouraged to monitor their children's online activities and help enforce this Privacy Policy.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own. We ensure adequate protection through:

  • Standard contractual clauses approved by the European Commission
  • Service providers with appropriate privacy certifications
  • Compliance with applicable international data protection laws
