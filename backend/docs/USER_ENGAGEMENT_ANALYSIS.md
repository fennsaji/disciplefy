## üß† **Psychology of Reading Engagement**

### Industry Research on Attention Spans

**General Online Content**:
- Average attention span: **8-12 minutes** (Microsoft Research, 2021)
- Mobile users: **3-5 minutes** (Nielsen Norman Group)
- Blog posts: Peak engagement at **7 minutes** (Medium data)

**Religious/Spiritual Content**:
- Daily devotionals: **5-7 minutes** optimal (YouVersion stats)
- Bible study apps: **70% of sessions < 10 minutes** (industry average)
- Deep study sessions: **15-20 minutes** max before fatigue

**Completion Rates by Duration**:
| Duration | Typical Completion Rate | User Psychology |
|----------|------------------------|-----------------|
| 1-3 min | 85-95% | "Quick win" - feels achievable |
| 4-7 min | 70-85% | Comfortable, sustainable daily habit |
| 8-12 min | 50-70% | Requires commitment, may abandon mid-way |
| 13-20 min | 30-50% | Only highly motivated users complete |
| 20+ min | 10-30% | High abandonment, "saved for later" |

### Standard Study (10 min) - ‚ö†Ô∏è BORDERLINE
**Current**: 10 minutes, 2,000-2,500 words

**Psychological Assessment**:
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Above average attention span threshold
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk of users "saving for later" (then forgetting)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è May feel like a commitment rather than a habit

- ‚ùå Too long for spontaneous mobile use

- 40-60% completion rate

**Competitive Analysis**:
- YouVersion plans of the day: 5-7 minutes
- She Reads Truth: 6-8 minutes
- Bible Project: 8-10 minutes (video + reading)

**Recommendation**: **REDUCE TO 5-7 MINUTES**
- New target: 1,000-1,400 words
- Better completion rates
- More sustainable daily habit
- Still provides depth vs Quick Read

### Deep Dive (25 min) - ‚ùå TOO LONG
**Current**: 25 minutes, 5,000-6,000 words

**Psychological Assessment**:
- ‚ùå Exceeds typical engagement threshold significantly
- ‚ùå Very high abandonment risk (70%+)
- ‚ùå Users unlikely to complete in one session
- ‚ùå "I'll do this on the weekend" syndrome (then never do)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è May intimidate users from even starting

**Reality Check**:
- 25 minutes = watching a TV episode
- Most users overestimate available time
- Scrolling fatigue sets in around 12-15 minutes
- Eye strain on mobile devices

**User Types Who MIGHT Complete**:
- Seminary students (academic requirement)
- Small group leaders preparing for teaching
- Retired individuals with dedicated study time
- ~5-10% of user base

**Alternative User Behaviors**:
- Save and never return (60%)
- Skim/skip sections (25%)
- Abandon mid-way (10%)
- Actually complete (5%)

**Recommendation**: **REDUCE TO 12-15 MINUTES**
- New target: 2,400-3,000 words
- Still "deep" compared to Standard
- More realistic completion
- Can be done during lunch break or evening wind-down

---

**Current**: 15 minutes, 3,000-3,500 words

- ‚ö†Ô∏è Above optimal engagement window
- ‚úÖ Nature of Lectio (meditative) may allow longer engagement

- Could include guided pauses (extends clock time but not reading time)

**Recommendation**: **REDUCE TO 10-12 MINUTES**
- New target: 2,000-2,400 words
- Still allows for meditative depth
- More accessible to beginners
- Could add "Extended Lectio" as separate option later

## üí° **Alternative Strategies**

Instead of just reducing length, consider:

### 1. **Progressive Disclosure**
- Start with 3-min summary
- "Expand for more depth" button
- Users self-select deeper content
- Reduces initial overwhelm

### 2. **Checkpoint System**
- Break long studies into sections
- Save progress automatically
- "Resume where you left off"
- Reduces abandonment anxiety

### 3. **Speed Reading Options**
- "Quick version" vs "Full version" toggle
- AI-generated summaries for busy days
- Users choose depth based on available time

### 4. **Audio Companion**
- Text-to-speech for hands-free listening
- Users can "read" while commuting/exercising
- Extends viable use cases

### 5. **Hybrid Approach**
```
Quick Read: 3 min (keep as is)
Standard:
  - Condensed: 5 min
  - Full: 10 min (current)
Deep Dive:
  - Essential: 12 min
  - Extended: 25 min (current)
```
