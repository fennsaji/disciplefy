# Disciplefy Marketing Plan - India Launch

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** December 2025  
**Target Markets:** North India (Hindi-speaking) & Kerala (Malayalam-speaking)

---

## Executive Summary

Disciplefy is an AI-powered Bible study app designed to help Christians deepen their faith through personalized study guides, daily verses, and structured learning paths. This marketing plan outlines strategies to reach the North Indian and Malayali Christian communities through organic social media, paid advertising, and community partnerships.

**Primary Goals:**
- 10,000 app downloads in first 3 months
- 2,500 active weekly users
- 500 premium subscribers
- Build community presence across 5+ platforms

## 1. Target Audience Analysis

### 1.1 North Indian Christians (Hindi-Speaking)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 18-45 years
- States: Delhi NCR, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab
- Languages: Hindi, English
- Population: ~30 million Christians in North India

**Psychographics:**
- First/second generation Christians seeking deeper Biblical knowledge
- Young professionals balancing faith with busy schedules
- Students looking for accessible Bible study resources
- Parents wanting to teach children about faith
- Church leaders seeking study materials

**Pain Points:**
- Limited Hindi Bible study resources
- Difficulty maintaining consistent devotional habits
- Lack of structured learning paths
- Language barriers with English-only apps
- Expensive seminary/Bible study courses

**Digital Behavior:**
- Heavy Facebook & WhatsApp usage
- Growing Instagram adoption (18-35 age group)
- YouTube for worship music & sermons
- ShareChat for regional content

### 1.2 Malayali Christians (Kerala)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 18-55 years
- Location: Kerala, Gulf countries (UAE, Saudi, Kuwait, Qatar), US, UK, Canada
- Languages: Malayalam, English
- Population: ~6 million in Kerala + ~3 million diaspora

**Denominations:**
- Syrian Orthodox (Jacobite, Orthodox)
- Catholic (Latin, Syro-Malabar, Syro-Malankara)
- Marthoma
- CSI (Church of South India)
- Pentecostal/Charismatic

**Psychographics:**
- Strong church community ties
- High literacy rate (94%+)
- Tech-savvy population
- Strong emphasis on education & spiritual growth
- Active in church activities and Bible study groups

**Pain Points:**
- Want deeper theological understanding
- Busy professionals need flexible study times
- Diaspora seeks connection to faith traditions
- Youth need engaging, modern Bible study tools

**Digital Behavior:**
- Very active on Facebook (especially 30+)
- Instagram popular among youth
- WhatsApp groups for church communities
- YouTube for sermons & worship
- High smartphone penetration

## 4. Paid Advertising Strategy

### 4.1 Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram)

#### Campaign Structure

**Campaign 1: Awareness (Top of Funnel)**
- Objective: Brand Awareness / Reach
- Budget: 30% of ad spend
- Targeting:
  - Interest: Christianity, Bible, Jesus, Church, Prayer
  - Behaviors: Mobile app installers
  - Demographics: 18-55, Hindi/Malayalam speakers
  - Locations: India (focus states), Gulf countries
- Creative: Video ads showing app features, testimonials

**Campaign 2: Consideration (Middle of Funnel)**
- Objective: App Installs / Traffic
- Budget: 50% of ad spend
- Targeting:
  - Custom audiences: Website visitors, video viewers
  - Lookalike audiences: Based on app installers
  - Interest layering: Bible study + Mobile apps
- Creative: Feature demos, user testimonials, free trial offers

**Campaign 3: Conversion (Bottom of Funnel)**
- Objective: App Installs with optimization for in-app events
- Budget: 20% of ad spend
- Targeting:
  - Retargeting: App installers who haven't subscribed
  - High-intent lookalikes
- Creative: Premium feature highlights, limited-time offers

#### Audience Segments

**Segment 1: Young Believers (18-30)**
```
Interests: Contemporary Christian music, Hillsong, Bethel Music, 
           Youth ministry, Campus Crusade
Behaviors: Engaged shoppers, Early technology adopters
Languages: Hindi, Malayalam, English
```

**Segment 2: Church Leaders & Teachers (30-55)**
```
Interests: Theology, Seminary, Bible teaching, Pastoral ministry,
           Sunday school, Christian education
Job Titles: Pastor, Minister, Teacher, Church leader
Languages: Hindi, Malayalam, English
```

**Segment 3: Parents & Families (25-45)**
```
Interests: Christian parenting, Family devotions, Children's Bible,
           Homeschooling, Family values
Behaviors: Parents with children (all ages)
Languages: Hindi, Malayalam, English
```

**Segment 4: Gulf Diaspora (25-50)**
```
Locations: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar, Oman, Bahrain
Interests: Indian Christian community, Malayalam church,
           Hindi worship, Expatriate groups
Languages: Malayalam, Hindi, English
```

#### Ad Creative Guidelines

**Video Ads (15-30 seconds)**
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Show app interface clearly
- Include captions (sound-off viewing)
- End with clear CTA: "Download Free"
- Aspect ratios: 9:16 (Stories/Reels), 1:1 (Feed), 16:9 (In-stream)

**Image Ads**
- Clean, minimalist design
- App screenshots with device mockups
- Text overlay: Key benefit + CTA
- Brand colors consistent
- Languages: Create separate for Hindi, Malayalam, English

## Appendix C: Brand Guidelines for Marketing

### Voice & Tone
- **Warm & Welcoming**: Like a friend sharing faith
- **Knowledgeable but Accessible**: Expert content, simple language
- **Encouraging**: Always uplifting, never condemning
- **Authentic**: Real stories, genuine engagement

### Visual Guidelines
- **Colors**: Use brand primary indigo (#4F46E5), gold accent (#FFEEC0)
- **Typography**: Clean, readable fonts
- **Imagery**: Diverse Indian Christians, warm lighting
- **Style**: Modern, minimalist, faith-inspired

### Do's & Don'ts
**Do:**
- Celebrate diverse Christian traditions
- Use Scripture accurately with proper context
- Engage respectfully with all denominations
- Share authentic user stories

**Don't:**
- Criticize other religions or denominations
- Make exaggerated claims
- Use manipulative marketing tactics
- Share content that could be divisive
